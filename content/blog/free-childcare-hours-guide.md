# Free Childcare Hours 2026: Everything You Need to Know

The UK government offers significant free childcare entitlements for families with young children. But the system is not always easy to navigate. Different entitlements kick in at different ages, eligibility rules vary, and the application process involves multiple steps. This guide explains everything clearly so you can claim every hour your family is entitled to.

## The Four Main Entitlements

In 2026, there are four distinct funded childcare entitlements in England. Each has its own eligibility criteria and start date.

### 1. 15 Hours for Working Parents of Children Aged 9 Months to 2 Years

Eligible working parents can access 15 hours of funded childcare per week (570 hours per year) from the term after their child turns nine months old.

**Eligibility:** Both parents (or a single parent) must be working and each earning at least the equivalent of 16 hours per week at the National Minimum Wage. Neither parent can earn more than £100,000 per year individually.

**When it starts:** The term after your child turns nine months. The three terms start in September, January, and April.

### 2. 30 Hours for Working Parents of Two-Year-Olds

From the term after your child turns two, eligible working parents can access 30 hours of funded childcare per week (1,140 hours per year).

**Eligibility:** Same income criteria as above — both parents working, each earning between the National Minimum Wage equivalent for 16 hours and £100,000 per year.

**When it starts:** The term after your child turns two.

### 3. 15 Hours Universal Entitlement for All Three and Four-Year-Olds

Every three and four-year-old in England is entitled to 15 hours of free childcare per week (570 hours per year), regardless of family income or employment status.

**Eligibility:** Universal — no income or work requirements. Your child qualifies simply by being three or four years old.

**When it starts:** The term after your child turns three.

### 4. 30 Hours for Working Parents of Three and Four-Year-Olds

Working parents of three and four-year-olds can access an additional 15 hours on top of the universal entitlement, giving a total of 30 hours per week (1,140 hours per year).

**Eligibility:** Same income criteria as the other working parent entitlements.

**When it starts:** The term after your child turns three, if you already have a valid 30-hour code.

## Who Qualifies as a "Working Parent"?

For the working parent entitlements, you must meet all of the following:

- **Minimum earnings:** Each parent (or the sole parent in a single-parent household) must earn at least £183.82 per week (equivalent to 16 hours at the National Minimum Wage for those aged 21 and over). This figure is updated annually.
- **Maximum earnings:** Neither parent can earn more than £100,000 per year individually. This is assessed on individual income, not household income.
- **Employment status:** You can be employed, self-employed, or on a zero-hours contract. If your income fluctuates, you qualify as long as you expect to meet the minimum earnings threshold on average.

### Exceptions

You may still qualify even if one parent is not working, if that parent:

- Is on maternity, paternity, adoption, or shared parental leave
- Is unable to work due to disability or caring responsibilities (with Carer's Allowance)
- Has a partner who is working and meets the income criteria, while the non-working parent receives certain benefits

If your situation is complex, use the [GOV.UK Childcare Choices eligibility checker](https://www.childcarechoices.gov.uk) for a definitive answer.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

### Step 1: Check Your Eligibility

Visit the [GOV.UK Childcare Choices website](https://www.childcarechoices.gov.uk) and use the eligibility checker. You will need details of your income, employment, and any benefits you receive.

### Step 2: Create a Childcare Service Account

If you do not already have one, create an account on the [Childcare Service website](https://www.gov.uk/apply-free-childcare-if-youre-working). You will need your National Insurance number and your child's details.

### Step 3: Apply for Your Code

For the working parent entitlements (15 hours from 9 months, 30 hours from age 2, and 30 hours for 3–4 year olds), you will receive an eligibility code. This code is what your nursery needs to claim the funded hours on your behalf.

For the universal 15 hours for three and four-year-olds, you do not need a code — your nursery will claim this automatically once your child is eligible.

### Step 4: Share Your Code with Your Nursery

Give your eligibility code and your National Insurance number to your nursery. They will verify it through the local authority system and apply the funded hours to your account.

### Step 5: Reconfirm Every Three Months

Your eligibility for working parent entitlements must be reconfirmed every three months through your Childcare Service account. Set a reminder — if you miss the deadline, you risk losing your funded hours for the following term.

## Key Dates and Deadlines

Funded hours operate on a termly basis. To start your funded place at the beginning of a term, you must have a valid eligibility code by the following deadlines:

| Term | Funded hours start | Code must be valid by |
|------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Autumn (September) | First week of September | 31 August |
| Spring (January) | First week of January | 31 December |
| Summer (April) | First week of April | 31 March |

Apply well in advance. Processing can take up to two weeks, and delays near the deadline can mean waiting a full term for your hours to start.

## How Funded Hours Work in Practice

### Can I Use Them at Any Nursery?

Not necessarily. Nurseries choose whether to offer funded places, and some have a limited number available. When searching for nurseries on [NurseryMatch](/search), check whether each setting offers funded hours. It is one of the most important factors when shortlisting nurseries.

### Can I Split Hours Between Settings?

Yes. You can use your funded hours across two different providers — for example, 15 hours at a nursery and 15 hours with a childminder. Both providers need your eligibility code.

### Can I Use Funded Hours Flexibly?

Funded hours must be used within a maximum of 10 hours per day, across a minimum of two days per week. Within those limits, you and your nursery can agree a pattern that works for both of you.

Some nurseries offer "stretched" funding, where the annual hours are spread across 50 or 51 weeks (rather than 38 term-time weeks). This gives you fewer hours per week but covers holiday periods too. Ask your nursery which model they use.

### Are There Extra Charges?

Nurseries cannot charge for funded hours themselves. However, they can charge for additional services like meals, nappies, consumables, and any hours beyond the funded entitlement. Some nurseries require funded children to attend a minimum number of additional paid hours. This is legal but should be made clear upfront.

If a nursery is charging in a way that feels unfair, contact your local authority's Family Information Service for guidance.

## Funded Childcare in Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland

The entitlements described above apply to England. Funded childcare differs across the UK:

**Scotland:** All three to five-year-olds (and eligible two-year-olds) receive 1,140 hours of funded childcare per year — equivalent to 30 hours per week during term time. This is universal and not means-tested for three to five-year-olds.

**Wales:** All three and four-year-olds receive a minimum of 10 hours per week of free early education. The Childcare Offer for Wales provides an additional 20 hours of funded childcare for working parents of three and four-year-olds, giving 30 hours total.

**Northern Ireland:** Pre-school education is funded for the year before a child starts school (typically age three to four), offering 12.5 hours per week. There is currently no equivalent of England's 30-hour working parent entitlement.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Applying too late.** Allow at least four weeks before the term start date to ensure your code is processed.
- **Forgetting to reconfirm.** Set calendar reminders for your quarterly reconfirmation. A lapse can cost you an entire term of funding.
- **Not checking nursery availability.** Do not assume every nursery offers funded places. Confirm before committing.
- **Ignoring additional costs.** Funded hours cover the education and childcare, not extras. Budget for meals and other charges separately.
- **Missing the two-year-old entitlement.** Many families do not realise that 30 funded hours are available from age two for working parents. Check and apply early.
