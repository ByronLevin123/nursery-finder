# Best Areas for Families in the UK: 2026 Guide

Choosing where to live as a family involves weighing up dozens of factors: school quality, nursery availability, safety, green space, transport links, and of course affordability. This guide highlights some of the best areas for families across the UK in 2026, drawing on nursery density data, Ofsted ratings, crime statistics, and property price trends.

Whether you are relocating for work, upsizing for a growing family, or simply exploring your options, this guide will help you focus your search.

## How We Assessed Each Area

We looked at five key factors that matter most to families with young children:

1. **Nursery availability** — density of Ofsted-registered nurseries per 1,000 children under five
2. **Quality of early years provision** — percentage of nurseries rated Good or Outstanding by Ofsted
3. **Safety** — neighbourhood crime rates from publicly available police data
4. **Green space and outdoor access** — parks, playgrounds, and nature within walking distance
5. **Affordability** — average property prices relative to local earnings

No single area tops every category. The best place for your family depends on your priorities and budget. Use the [NurseryMatch area pages](/areas) to explore detailed profiles for any area you are considering.

## Top Areas in England

### Harrogate, North Yorkshire

Harrogate consistently ranks among the best places to live in the UK, and for good reason. The town has an excellent range of nurseries, with over 85% rated Good or Outstanding. Crime rates are well below the national average, and the surrounding countryside offers endless outdoor space. Property prices are above the Yorkshire average but significantly below equivalent areas in the South East.

**Best for:** Families who want a high quality of life outside the major cities, with excellent early years provision and low crime.

### Bath, Somerset

Bath combines a thriving cultural scene with strong family infrastructure. Nursery density is high, and the city benefits from a large number of Outstanding-rated settings. The surrounding villages offer more affordable housing while remaining within easy reach of the city's amenities. Explore nurseries in [Bath](/nurseries-in/BA1) to see current availability.

**Best for:** Families who want city amenities without city stress, plus easy access to the countryside.

### St Albans, Hertfordshire

Just 20 minutes from central London by train, St Albans offers the best of both worlds. Nursery provision is excellent, primary schools are strong, and the city has a village feel despite its commuter connections. Property prices are high, reflecting demand, but families benefit from London salaries with a lower cost of living than the capital.

**Best for:** London commuters who want their children to grow up with more space and a strong community feel.

### Exeter, Devon

Exeter has seen significant family-friendly development over the past decade. Nursery availability is good, crime rates are low, and the city offers easy access to both Dartmoor and the Devon coast. Property prices remain more affordable than many southern cities.

**Best for:** Families who prioritise outdoor lifestyle and affordability without sacrificing urban amenities.

### Jesmond, Newcastle upon Tyne

Jesmond is one of the most sought-after family areas in the North East. Tree-lined streets, independent shops, and a strong sense of community make it a popular choice. Nursery provision is good, with several Outstanding settings within walking distance. Property prices are a fraction of equivalent London neighbourhoods.

**Best for:** Families seeking excellent value, a vibrant community, and good transport links.

## Top Areas in London

### Battersea, SW11

Battersea has long been a magnet for young families. Nursery density is among the highest in London, and the area benefits from Battersea Park, excellent transport links, and a strong community feel. Property prices are high, but the range of nursery options provides genuine choice. Browse nurseries in [Battersea](/nurseries-in/SW11).

### Dulwich, SE21

Dulwich offers a village atmosphere within London, with tree-lined streets, Dulwich Park, and a strong network of nurseries and schools. The proportion of Outstanding-rated nurseries is well above the London average. View nurseries in [Dulwich](/nurseries-in/SE21).

### Muswell Hill, N10

Muswell Hill is a popular family area in North London with excellent nursery provision, independent shops, and proximity to Alexandra Park. Crime rates are lower than the London average, and the community feel is strong.

### Chiswick, W4

West London's Chiswick combines good transport links with a leafy, family-friendly environment. Nursery availability is strong, and the area has several highly rated settings. Check nurseries in [Chiswick](/nurseries-in/W4).

## Top Areas in Scotland

### Stockbridge, Edinburgh

Stockbridge is one of Edinburgh's most desirable family areas. It has a village feel, excellent independent shops, and direct access to the Water of Leith walkway and Inverleith Park. Scotland's generous funded childcare provision (1,140 hours per year for three to five-year-olds) makes nursery significantly more affordable here than in much of England.

### Bearsden, Glasgow

Bearsden consistently ranks among the best places for families in Scotland. Primary schools are excellent, crime rates are low, and the area offers easy access to the Campsie Fells for outdoor activities. Nursery provision is strong, with a good mix of local authority and private settings.

## Top Areas in Wales

### Pontcanna, Cardiff

Pontcanna is Cardiff's most popular family neighbourhood, with tree-lined streets, Pontcanna Fields, and a short walk to Bute Park. Nursery availability is good, and Wales offers 30 hours of free early education for three and four-year-olds. Property prices are reasonable compared to equivalent English cities.

## What to Check Before You Move

Before committing to a move, dig into the details:

1. **Search for nurseries.** Use the [NurseryMatch search tool](/search) to see exactly what nursery provision is available in your target area. Filter by Ofsted rating and distance to understand the real picture, not just the marketing.

2. **Check crime data.** The [NurseryMatch area pages](/areas) include neighbourhood crime data drawn from the Police UK API, giving you an honest picture of local safety.

3. **Look at school catchment areas.** If you are planning ahead, check the primary school catchment for any property you are considering. Catchment areas can be very tight in popular areas.

4. **Assess transport links.** If one or both parents commute, factor in the real daily journey time. A beautiful area loses its appeal if the commute is three hours a day.

5. **Visit at different times.** Visit your target area on a weekday morning, a weekend afternoon, and an evening. Each gives you a different perspective on what living there is actually like.

6. **Talk to local parents.** Nobody knows an area better than the families already living there. Ask about nursery waiting lists, school quality, and the things that do not show up in statistics.

## Affordability Matters

The best area for your family is one you can afford without financial stress. A long commute to fund a bigger house in a better postcode may not be worth it when you factor in the impact on family time. Similarly, stretching your budget to the limit for a premium area leaves no room for unexpected costs.

Consider the total cost of living, not just property prices. Nursery fees vary significantly by area — use our [nursery costs guide](/blog/nursery-costs-uk-2026) for a regional breakdown. Factor in council tax, transport costs, and the availability of funded childcare hours.
